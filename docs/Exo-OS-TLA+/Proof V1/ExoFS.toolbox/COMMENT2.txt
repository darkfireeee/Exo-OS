### What Does the "Fingerprint" Warning Mean?

You are right to be eagle-eyed about those probability estimates, but they are not an error. They are a mathematical reality of how TLA+ manages your computer's RAM.

Here is what is happening under the hood:
* **The Memory Problem:** Storing the full, complete map of 14.4 million distinct states would take up massive amounts of RAM (well beyond the 4GB you allocated with `-Xmx4g`).
* **The Fingerprint Solution:** Instead of storing the whole state, TLC calculates a 64-bit "fingerprint" (a hash) of each state and stores *that*. 
* **The Catch (Hash Collisions):** Because TLC is mapping giant states into 64-bit numbers, there is a tiny, microscopic chance that two completely different states accidentally generate the exact same fingerprint. If this happens, TLC thinks, *"Oh, I've already evaluated this state,"* and skips it, potentially missing a branch of the state tree.
